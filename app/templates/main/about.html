COVID-19’s largest impact is felt in hospital systems, among healthcare workers and within affected families, but the next biggest battle is the unemployed workforce. As health care workers, scientists, politicians, and governments fight on the front lines, there is a second war in this battle and its impact on the world will be even more drastic.
Millions across the globe have lost jobs. Industries are collapsing. Companies are closing. The world economy is facing a crisis unlike any experienced in modern history. The Coronavirus pandemic has changed the life of every human being on this planet. Since March 2020 when the global virus hit the Earth, official registered unemployment numbers continue to rise and those in need of work continues to exponentially grow.
Brighter days ahead will be ensured by revitalizing the economy, allowing all people impacted by the unemployment crisis to return to work, to discover professional aspirations and to overcome hardships posed by the Coronavirus. As the world finds a balance between maintaining safety and returning to normalcy, new industries will emerge that require creativity and innovation.
Community joined by shared experience, mutual challenges, even common tragedy is what we yearn for today. Knowing that we do not face the world alone provides immense comfort in unprecedented times and ultimately provides the the necessary strength to persevere.

Meet the ILMJTCV team.


Gilad Spitzer is a recent graduate of Shalhevet High School in California. He will be studying computer science and business next year at the University of Michigan. Though only 18 years old, he constantly ponders, “How can I use my computer science skills to help others?”
When his second-semester senior year was disrupted by Coronavirus, he redirected his focus to the many others around the globe affected in more serious ways. While he is not busy coding or serving his high school student body, you can find Gilad cooking gourmet meals or on a run on the sunny pacific coast.

Sigal Spitzer is an incoming associate at The Boston Consulting Group in New York. She recently graduated from The University of Pennsylvania and earned a masters degree in behavioral science. Sigal asks herself, “How can I use my skills and expertise to make an impact?”
When her start-date for her new job was postponed due to Coronavirus, she thought about millions of others encountering even more harsh unemployment setbacks and was determined to take action. When she is not running half marathons or acing a tennis serve, Sigal can be found baking delicious chocolate or fruity sweets.

Zachary Flamholz is an MdPhD student at Einstein Medical College in New York. After graduating from Princeton University, he worked at The University of Pennsylvania conducting medical research for a year. He often finds himself asking, “How can I help those around me to make the world a better place?”
As Coronavirus moved his courses online, Zachary fervently followed the ever changing news stories and became passionate about helping the unemployed by providing resources to find local employment opportunities. When he is not conducting quantitative medical research or studying for his exams, you can find Zachary putting on the golf course or watching The Office.
